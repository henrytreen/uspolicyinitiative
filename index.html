What this is

A practical, accessible introduction to U.S. civics, law, and public policy—designed for students who didn’t grow up with the U.S. system.

  • Explain the structure: Constitution, federalism, separation of powers
  • Demystify lawmaking: Congress, the presidency, agencies, courts
  • Use real examples: landmark cases + modern policy debates

How we run sessions

Programs are typically 45–60 minutes, once per week, for 4–8 weeks. Each session includes a short lesson + a case study + discussion.

  • Live on Zoom / Google Meet
  • Optional recording + handouts
  • Discussion prompts for clubs/classrooms

Core curriculum (example 6-session series)

1) Foundations

Constitution, rights, federal vs. state power.

2) Congress

Committees, parties, compromise, how a bill becomes law.

3) The President

Executive orders, agencies, limits on executive power.

4) The Courts

Judicial review, constitutional interpretation, key cases.

5) Policy in Action

How issues become policy: tradeoffs, evidence, incentives.

6) Influence

Media, lobbying, voting, activism, and civic engagement.
